To flash an assembled PCB, use the \texttt{STDC-STM32-rs} submodule. 
There is a \texttt{DEBUGGING.md} that explains how to flash and test the software.
The command runner \lstinline{just} is recommended to easily run the predefined tasks, e.g. \lstinline{just features=bluetooth,display embed}, \lstinline{just embed-live-sim-log-DEPTH}.

To view and update the diving algorithms, update the \texttt{STDC-thalmann} submodule and use the updated dependency version in the firmware crate's \texttt{Cargo.toml}.

\chapter{Additional Figures: \gls{pcb} Design}
The following figures are screenshots from the KiCad software, which has been used for the \gls{pcb} design.
The first and third layer are displayed here, the second and fourth layers only provide a ground reference, which is cut where VIAs are used to travel other signals than ground between layers 1 and 3.

The \gls{mcu} is the \gls{lqfp}-component with 64 leads in the center of the \gls{pcb}, in the top left corner, the battery connector and power management section can be seen. 
In the lower left hand corner is the display connector and required DC-DC converters, capacitors and \gls{mcu} \gls{gpio} connections.

On the right side, from the top to the bottom, there are the UART pads, the pressure sensor, debug LEDs next to SWD pads, flash chip and the Bluetooth module.
